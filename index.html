About Me

Albert Einstein (1879-1955) was a renowned German-born physicist celebrated for his theory of relativity and the iconic equation E=mcÂ². In 1905, his "miracle year," he published groundbreaking papers on the photoelectric effect, Brownian motion,ch and special relativity, which revolutionized physics. Later, he proposed general relativity, reshaping our understanding of gravity and cosmology. Einstein also contributed to quantum physics and introduced the concept of photons. While he sought to unify physics and opposed quantum randomness, these efforts led to his isolation in the scientific community. Einstein fled Nazi Germany, became a U.S. citizen, and played a role in alerting the U.S. to the German nuclear program during World War II.
